Understanding Claude

What is Claude?

An AI Assistant Built by Anthropic

Claude is an artificial intelligence assistant created by Anthropic, a company founded in 2021 by former members of OpenAI. Think of Claude as an extremely knowledgeable colleague who can read, write, analyse, reason, and code — but who needs human oversight and cannot physically do anything in the real world.

When you type a message to Claude, it processes your text and generates a response. It does not search the internet in real time (unless specifically connected to a search tool). Instead, it draws on a vast amount of training data — books, articles, code, and other text — to understand and respond to your request.

How Does It Compare?

Claude vs ChatGPT vs Gemini

You have probably heard of ChatGPT (made by OpenAI) and Gemini (made by Google). All three are large language models (LLMs) — AI systems trained on huge amounts of text data. Here is how they compare:

Feature Claude (Anthropic) ChatGPT (OpenAI) Gemini (Google)
Best at Long documents, nuanced reasoning, careful analysis, coding General chat, image generation, broad knowledge Google integration, multimodal tasks, search
Safety approach Constitutional AI — built-in ethical guidelines RLHF — human feedback training Google's internal safety framework
Context window Up to 200,000 tokens (approx. 500 pages) Up to 128,000 tokens Up to 1 million tokens
Why Hillway chose it Best for property reports, analysis, and professional writing — —

Why Hillway uses Claude: For property work, Claude consistently produces the most professional, nuanced, and accurate output. It handles long documents (like leases and reports) exceptionally well, its reasoning is transparent, and it follows instructions precisely. This matters when your output goes to RICS-regulated clients.

Capabilities

What Claude Can Do

Claude is not just a chatbot. It is a multi-purpose tool that can help with a wide range of tasks:

Writing — Reports, emails, proposals, summaries
Analysis — Data interpretation, market research, comparables
Coding — Building software, automating tasks, fixing bugs
Reasoning — Working through complex problems step by step
Vision — Reading images, floor plans, screenshots, documents
Summarisation — Condensing long documents into key points
Translation — Converting between languages
Calculation — Financial models, yield analysis, benchmarking

Claude comes in three tiers, named after types of poetry. Each balances speed, cost, and intelligence differently. Think of it like choosing between a junior, mid-level, and senior surveyor — each is right for different tasks.

Access Points

How to Access Claude

There are three main ways to interact with Claude:

claude.ai (Web Interface) — Go to claude.ai in your browser. This is the simplest way to use Claude. Type your question, get an answer. You can upload files, share images, and have extended conversations. This is what you will use most often.

Claude Code (Terminal/CLI) — A command-line tool for developers. Matt uses this to build Hillway's software products. You will learn about it in Module 2. You do not need to use it day to day, but understanding what it does helps you see how our tools are built.

API (Application Programming Interface) — Allows software to talk to Claude programmatically. This is how Hillway's products (like Steer and Finlo) use Claude behind the scenes. You do not need to use the API directly.

Key Concept

The Conversation Model

When you chat with Claude, you are having a conversation that exists within a context window. Here is what that means in plain English:

  • Context window — The amount of text Claude can "hold in its head" at once. Claude can process roughly 200,000 tokens, which is approximately 500 pages of text. This is why it is brilliant at analysing long leases or reports.
  • System prompt — Instructions given to Claude before the conversation starts. At Hillway, we give Claude background on the firm, our clients, and how we want it to behave. It is like briefing a new colleague before a meeting.
  • Memory — Within a single conversation, Claude remembers everything that has been said. But when you start a new conversation, it starts fresh with no memory of previous chats (unless we connect it to a memory tool).
  • Tokens — The units Claude uses to measure text. One token is roughly three-quarters of a word. "Commercial property valuation" is about four tokens.

Responsibility

Safety and Responsible Use

Claude is designed with safety at its core. Anthropic uses an approach called Constitutional AI, which means Claude has been trained to be helpful, harmless, and honest. However, you should be aware of its limitations:

  • Hallucinations — Claude can sometimes present incorrect information confidently. This is called a "hallucination." Always verify important facts, especially property data, case law references, and financial figures.
  • Knowledge cutoff — Claude's training data has a cutoff date. It may not know about very recent events, new regulations, or the latest market data unless connected to live tools.
  • No professional qualifications — Claude is not a surveyor, solicitor, or accountant. It can help draft work, but a qualified professional must review and sign off.
  • Confidentiality — Be thoughtful about what client data you share with Claude. We will cover this in detail in Module 5.

Claude Code Basics

What is It?

Claude Code: AI in the Terminal

Claude Code is Anthropic's official command-line interface (CLI) for Claude. If the claude.ai website is like having a conversation over email, Claude Code is like having Claude sitting next to you at your desk, able to read your files, edit your code, run commands, and build things directly on your computer.

You do not need to become a Claude Code expert. But understanding what it is and what it can do helps you see how Hillway's digital products are built, and may inspire ideas for automating your own work.

Key Difference

Claude Code vs claude.ai

Here is the simplest way to understand the difference:

Feature claude.ai (Web) Claude Code (CLI)
Interface Browser-based chat window Terminal / command line
Who uses it Everyone Developers and technical users
Can read files Only if you upload them Yes — directly from your computer
Can edit files No Yes — creates, edits, and deletes files
Can run commands No Yes — runs terminal commands, builds software
Can connect to tools Limited (via projects) Yes — Xero, Google Workspace, GitHub, databases, and more
Best for Quick questions, writing, analysis Building software, automation, system administration

Getting Started

Installation and Setup

Claude Code runs in the terminal (the text-based interface on a Mac or PC). Here is what the setup process looks like:

Terminal # Install Claude Code (one-time setup) $ npm install -g @anthropic-ai/claude-code # Start Claude Code in a project directory $ cd ~/Projects/my-project $ claude Claude Code v1.0 Ready to help. What would you like to do? # Now you can type requests in plain English > Read the file report.docx and summarise the key findings

You do not need to memorise these commands. The key takeaway is that Claude Code is installed once, then you simply type what you want in plain English — no coding syntax required.

Core Concepts

Basic Commands and Interactions

When you are inside Claude Code, you interact with it using natural language. Here are examples of the kinds of things you can ask:

Request
"Read the lease agreement in the documents folder and tell me the break clause dates"
Claude reads the file directly from the computer, analyses the lease, and extracts the relevant dates without you needing to upload anything.
Request
"Create a new spreadsheet with columns for property address, tenant, rent, and lease expiry"
Claude creates the file on your computer, ready to open and use.
Request
"Run the test suite and fix any failing tests"
Claude runs the tests, reads the error messages, identifies the problems, fixes the code, and re-runs the tests to verify — all autonomously.

Safety

The Permission Model

Claude Code has a permission system to keep you in control. By default, it will ask for your approval before doing anything that changes files or runs commands. Think of it as a co-pilot that checks with you before taking the controls.

  • Read-only actions — Claude can read files and search through code without asking permission
  • Write actions — Editing files, creating files, or deleting files requires your approval
  • System commands — Running terminal commands (like installing software) requires your approval
  • Trusted mode — Power users (like Matt) can pre-approve certain actions so Claude works more autonomously

External Connections

MCP: Model Context Protocol

One of Claude Code's most powerful features is MCP (Model Context Protocol). This is the technology that lets Claude connect to external tools and services. Think of it as giving Claude the ability to "plug in" to other systems.

At Hillway, Claude Code is connected to:

Xero — Our accounting software
Google Workspace — Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Calendar
GitHub — Where our code lives
Supabase — Our database platform
Slack — Team communication
Sentry — Error monitoring
Monday.com — Project management
Playwright — Browser automation

This means Claude can check our accounts in Xero, send emails through Gmail, create documents in Google Docs, and manage our codebase on GitHub — all from a single interface. It is like having an assistant with access to every tool in the office.

Day-to-Day Usage at Hillway

From Theory to Practice

Now that you understand what Claude is and how Claude Code works, let us look at how you will actually use AI in your day-to-day role as an apprentice surveyor. These are practical, real-world applications that will save you time and improve your output from week one.

Communication

Email Drafting

One of the most common uses of Claude at Hillway is drafting professional correspondence. As an apprentice, you will be writing emails to clients, agents, solicitors, and contractors. Claude helps you get the tone and content right.

Example Prompt
"Draft a professional email to the tenant at Unit 3 Riverside Business Park confirming that we have received their dilapidations claim and will be inspecting the property next Thursday at 10am. Keep the tone firm but polite."
Claude generates a complete, professional email with the right level of formality, appropriate caveats, and correct property terminology. You review it, adjust if needed, and send.

Pro tip: Always specify the tone you want (formal, friendly, firm, conciliatory) and any key points that must be included. The more context you give Claude, the better the output.

Research

Document Summarisation

Surveyors deal with long documents — leases, planning applications, building surveys, EPC reports, title deeds. Claude can read and summarise these in seconds.

Example Prompt
"I have uploaded a 40-page commercial lease. Please extract: (1) the tenant and landlord names, (2) the lease term and break dates, (3) the rent and review provisions, (4) any unusual or onerous clauses, (5) the repair obligations."
Claude reads the entire document and provides a structured summary with page references. This turns a 2-hour reading task into a 5-minute review task.

Remember: Claude's summary is a starting point. You must still read the key clauses yourself, especially for client-facing advice. Use Claude to identify what matters, then verify it.

Intelligence

Property Research

When working on a new instruction, you need background on the property, the area, the tenant, and the market. Claude can help gather and structure this information.

Example Prompt
"I am preparing for a rent review at a 5,000 sq ft industrial unit in Doncaster. The current rent is £6.50 per sq ft. What should I know about the Doncaster industrial market? What comparable evidence would be relevant? What range of rents would you expect?"
Claude provides an overview of the local industrial market, suggests types of comparable evidence to look for, and gives indicative rent ranges based on its training data — with caveats about verifying with current market data.

Critical: Claude's market data may not be current. Always cross-reference with live sources — CoStar, EGi, Rightmove Commercial, or our own records. Claude gives you a head start; it does not replace proper market research.

Analysis

Market Analysis and Comparables

Claude can help you structure and analyse comparable evidence for valuations, rent reviews, and market appraisals.

Example Prompt
"I have five comparable lettings for retail units in Sheffield city centre. Here are the details [paste data]. Please create a comparison table, adjust for size and specification differences, and suggest an appropriate tone of value for a 2,000 sq ft unit on Division Street."
Claude creates a structured comparison, applies standard adjustment principles, and explains its reasoning. You then apply your own professional judgement to finalise the figure.

Writing

Report Drafting

Report writing is a significant part of surveying. Claude can help draft sections of reports, structure your findings, and ensure consistency of language and format.

Example Prompt
"Draft the property description section for a building survey report. The property is a two-storey detached industrial unit built circa 1985, steel portal frame construction, profile metal cladding, approximately 8,000 sq ft with a concrete yard. Located on Parkway Industrial Estate, Sheffield."
Claude generates a professionally worded property description following standard surveying conventions, which you then refine based on your actual site inspection notes.

Workflow: Inspect the property, take your own notes, then use Claude to turn those notes into polished prose. Never let Claude write about things you have not personally verified.

Xero is Hillway's accounting software. Through MCP connections, Claude can query Xero to pull financial data without you needing to log in and navigate the interface.

Claude Co-work & Agent Teams

Collaboration

What is Claude Co-work?

Claude Co-work is a feature within claude.ai that lets you create projects with shared context. Instead of starting every conversation from scratch, you can set up a project with background documents, instructions, and files that Claude remembers across all conversations within that project.

Think of it as giving Claude a permanent briefing folder for a specific topic or client. Every time you open a conversation in that project, Claude already knows the background.

Example: Hillway Project
A "Client: ABC Properties" project might contain: the client's portfolio list, their preferred communication style, key contacts, recent correspondence, and standard terms. Every conversation in this project starts with Claude already knowing all of this.

Teamwork

Working with Claude as a Team Member

At Hillway, we do not just ask Claude questions — we treat it as a team member. This means giving it context about its role, our standards, and our expectations. Here is how that works in practice:

Set the context — Tell Claude who it is working for, what the project is, and what standards apply. For example: "You are assisting a RICS-regulated surveying practice in Sheffield. All output must be professional and suitable for client-facing work."

Assign clear tasks — Be specific about what you need. "Draft the executive summary" is better than "help with the report."

Review and iterate — Treat Claude's first draft as a first draft. Provide feedback: "Good, but make the tone more formal and add a paragraph about the building's condition."

Maintain continuity — Use Co-work projects to keep context across multiple conversations, so you do not have to repeat background information.

Advanced

Agent Teams: Multiple Claudes Working Together

One of Hillway's most advanced uses of AI is agent teams — multiple Claude instances working together on the same task, each with a specific role. Think of it as assembling a project team, but every member is an AI specialist.

When Matt runs a complex task, he can spin up a team where each "agent" focuses on a different aspect:

  • A Lead Surveyor agent coordinates the work
  • A Property Researcher agent gathers market data and comparables
  • A Report Writer agent drafts professional documentation
  • A Commercial Analyst agent runs financial models and calculations
  • A Client Liaison agent drafts correspondence and manages communication

These agents communicate with each other, share findings, and produce coordinated output. It is like having a full project team available instantly, 24 hours a day.

Real-World Agent Team in Action

Here is what happens when Matt runs /team for a new instruction:

All of this happens in minutes, not days. The output still requires human review and professional sign-off, but the heavy lifting is done.

Agent teams do not replace surveyors. They handle the 80% of work that is research, drafting, and formatting, so you can focus on the 20% that requires professional judgement, client relationships, and on-the-ground expertise.

Tips & Best Practices

Technique

Writing Effective Prompts for Claude

The quality of Claude's output depends heavily on the quality of your input. Here are the principles of effective prompting:

Be specific, not vague. "Draft a rent review letter for the tenant at Unit 5, Riverside Park, referencing the November 2025 review date and our proposed rent of £45,000 per annum" is far better than "write a rent review letter."

Provide context. Tell Claude who the audience is, what the document is for, and what tone to use. "This is for a sophisticated institutional landlord who expects detailed market analysis" produces very different output from "this is for a small local business owner."

Specify the format. Want a table? Say so. Want bullet points? Say so. Want a formal letter with "Dear Sir" and "Yours faithfully"? Say so. Claude follows formatting instructions precisely.

Give examples. If you have a template or a previous report you liked, share it with Claude and say "follow this style." This is one of the most effective techniques for getting consistent output.

Iterate. Your first prompt rarely produces the perfect result. Read the output, identify what needs changing, and refine: "Good, but make the executive summary shorter and add a risk section at the end."

The golden rule: If you would not give the same vague brief to a junior colleague and expect good results, do not give it to Claude either. Clear instructions produce clear output.

Judgement

When to Use AI vs Professional Judgement

AI is a powerful tool, but it has clear boundaries. Here is a practical guide for when to use Claude and when to rely on your own professional skills:

USE CLAUDE FOR

  • Drafting correspondence
  • Summarising long documents
  • Structuring reports
  • Market research starting points
  • Financial calculations
  • Data formatting and analysis
  • Template creation
  • Learning and revision

RELY ON PROFESSIONAL JUDGEMENT FOR

  • Final valuation opinions
  • Physical site inspections
  • Client relationship decisions
  • Interpreting unusual lease clauses
  • Assessing building condition
  • Negotiation strategy
  • Professional sign-off on reports
  • Ethical and conflict decisions

Standards

RICS Professional Standards and AI Usage

As a RICS-regulated firm, Hillway must adhere to strict professional standards when using AI. Here is what you need to know:

  • Transparency — If AI has been used in preparing a report or valuation, this should be disclosed where appropriate. RICS is developing guidance on this, and we follow best practice.
  • Professional responsibility — The qualified surveyor signing off the work takes full professional responsibility, regardless of whether AI assisted in the preparation. Using Claude does not reduce your professional obligations.
  • Competence — You must understand the output well enough to explain and defend it. If Claude produces an analysis you do not understand, do not use it — ask someone to explain it or research it yourself.
  • Confidentiality — Client data shared with AI tools must be treated with the same care as any other form of information handling. See the Data Security section below.
  • Objectivity — AI output must not introduce bias into professional work. If Claude's analysis seems to favour one party, examine why and adjust.

Data Security

What to Share and What Not to Share

This is critical. While Claude is a powerful tool, you must be thoughtful about what information you share:

SAFE TO SHARE

  • General property descriptions
  • Public market data
  • Your own draft text for review
  • Standard lease clause questions
  • General technical queries
  • Template structures

BE CAREFUL WITH

  • Client names and personal data
  • Confidential financial information
  • Pre-publication valuations
  • Passwords or access credentials
  • Privileged legal correspondence
  • Data subject to NDA

Rule of thumb: If you would not email the information to someone outside the firm, think twice before sharing it with a general AI tool. Hillway's internal AI tools (connected via Claude Code with MCP) have more controlled access, but the principle still applies.

Quality

Building on Claude's Output

Claude produces good first drafts, but great output requires human refinement. Here is the review checklist you should follow:

Accuracy check — Are the facts correct? Are property addresses, measurements, dates, and figures accurate? Claude can hallucinate specific details.

Tone check — Is the language appropriate for the audience? Does it sound like something Hillway would send?

Completeness check — Has Claude covered everything you asked for? Has it missed any important points?

Professionalism check — Is the format correct? Are headings, numbering, and references consistent? Does it follow Hillway's house style?

Personal touch — Add your own observations, site-specific details, and professional insights that only a human who was there can provide.

Growth

Staying Current as AI Evolves

AI is moving faster than any technology in history. The tools you learn today will be significantly more capable in six months. Here is how to stay ahead:

Final Assessment 10 questions covering all modules
Question 1 of 10
A client asks you to produce an urgent market report for Sheffield industrial units. What is the correct approach using Claude?
The correct approach combines AI efficiency with professional rigour: use Claude for the heavy lifting (structure, research, drafting), verify with current data, add professional insight, and have a qualified surveyor review before sending to the client.
Question 2 of 10
What is a "context window" in relation to Claude?
The context window is the amount of text Claude can "hold in its head" at once — approximately 200,000 tokens or 500 pages. This determines how much information Claude can consider when generating a response.
Question 3 of 10
You are using Claude to draft a building survey report. Which of the following should you NOT rely on Claude for?
Assessing the physical condition of a building requires a human surveyor to physically inspect the property. Claude cannot see, touch, or smell a building. It can help you write about what you found, but the inspection itself must be done in person.
Question 4 of 10
Which of these is the best prompt for Claude?
Option (a) is the best prompt because it includes role assignment (RICS surveyor), specific details (property, unit, date), clear task (formal letter confirming appointment), and tone guidance (professional and formal).
Question 5 of 10
A colleague shares a confidential pre-publication valuation figure with you. Should you input this into Claude on claude.ai?
Pre-publication valuation figures are highly sensitive and should not be shared with general AI tools. This data could be market-moving and sharing it could breach confidentiality obligations. Use professional judgement about what information to share.
Question 6 of 10
What technology does Claude use to connect to external tools like Xero and Google Workspace?
MCP (Model Context Protocol) is the technology that lets Claude connect to external tools and services. It is like a universal adapter that allows Claude to "plug in" to Xero, Google Workspace, GitHub, and many other systems.
Question 7 of 10
In Hillway's agent team model, who takes final professional responsibility for the work?
Regardless of how much AI assists in preparation, the qualified human surveyor who reviews and signs off the work takes full professional responsibility. This is a fundamental RICS principle that AI does not change.
Question 8 of 10
You notice Claude has included a rental comparable in your report that seems too high. What should you do?
You should verify against live market data and correct or remove any inaccurate information. Claude can hallucinate specific figures, and you are professionally responsible for every piece of data in your report.
Question 9 of 10
What does Anthropic's "Constitutional AI" approach aim to make Claude?
Constitutional AI trains Claude to be helpful, harmless, and honest. These three principles guide Claude's behaviour: it aims to be useful, avoid causing harm, and be truthful about what it knows and does not know.
Question 10 of 10
You find a repetitive task in your daily work that could be automated with Claude. What should you do?
At Hillway, every workflow you identify for automation is valuable. Tell Matt — it could become an automated internal process or even a SaaS product. This "SaaS mindset" is central to how Hillway operates as both a surveying practice and a PropTech company.
